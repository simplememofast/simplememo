Definition

Spaced Repetition is a learning method based on the "forgetting curve" discovered by Hermann Ebbinghaus in 1885. After learning new information, you review it just before your retention drops, gradually extending intervals from 1 day тЖТ 3 days тЖТ 7 days тЖТ 14 days тЖТ 30 days. This transfers information to long-term memory with minimal study time. SRS tools like Anki and SuperMemo automate the review schedule.

What is Spaced Repetition?

A learning method based on the forgetting curve. By reviewing material just before you'd forget it and gradually increasing intervals, you build long-term memory with fewer study sessions.

What subjects work best with Spaced Repetition?

Vocabulary, medical/legal terminology, exam facts, historical dates тАФ any fact-based information that requires accurate recall. It excels where precise memory matters more than conceptual understanding.

Can I use Simple Memo for Spaced Repetition?

Yes. Send study material via Simple Memo, then use Gmail's snooze to resurface notes at 1 day, 3 days, 7 days intervals. This creates a lightweight SRS with much faster capture than flashcard apps.
