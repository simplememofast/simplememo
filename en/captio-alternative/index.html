What Happened to Captio? A Complete Timeline

Captio was one of the most popular "email yourself" apps on iOS, beloved by productivity enthusiasts for its dead-simple interface. Here is the full story of its rise and fall.

2013 -- Launch
Captio launched on the iOS App Store as a minimalist "send to self" email app. Its premise was simple: open the app, type a thought, tap send. The memo goes straight to your Gmail inbox. No folders, no categories, no formatting toolbar. The app gained a devoted following among GTD practitioners, journalists, and anyone who used their inbox as a capture tool.
2014-2019 -- Peak Popularity
Captio was featured by major productivity blogs including Lifehacker, The Sweet Setup, and MacStories. It maintained a 4.5+ star rating and became a staple recommendation in "best productivity apps" roundups. The app operated on a one-time purchase model at $2.99, making it accessible to almost everyone.
2020-2022 -- Google API Policy Changes
Google began progressively tightening its API access policies. New OAuth consent screen requirements, stricter scope limitations, and mandatory security assessments created growing compliance challenges. For a small development team maintaining a one-time-purchase app with no recurring revenue, these requirements became increasingly difficult to meet.
2023 -- App Store Removal
Captio was removed from the App Store. While no official statement was issued by the developers, the removal coincided with another round of Google API policy changes. Users who already had the app installed could continue using it temporarily, but new downloads were no longer possible.
2024-2025 -- Server Shutdown
Captio's backend servers were eventually shut down, rendering even existing installations non-functional. Users across Reddit, Twitter, and productivity forums began searching for alternatives. Many tried workarounds with Apple Shortcuts, the native Mail app, or general-purpose note apps -- but none replicated the Captio experience.
2026 -- Simple Memo Fills the Gap
Simple Memo launched with architecture specifically designed to avoid Captio's failure points: independent email infrastructure (no Gmail API dependency), a sustainable freemium business model, and end-to-end encryption. Former Captio users report that the transition feels seamless.
Key Lesson: Captio's shutdown was not caused by a lack of demand -- it was caused by architectural dependency. Building on top of Gmail's API meant that Google's policy changes could (and did) kill the product overnight. Simple Memo was designed from day one to avoid this single point of failure.

Why Captio Shut Down: Two Root Causes

Understanding why Captio failed helps you evaluate whether its replacement will last. There were two fundamental issues that doomed Captio, and Simple Memo's architecture was designed to address both.

1. Gmail API Dependency
Captio relied entirely on Gmail's API to send memos as emails. This seemed like a reasonable choice in 2013, but Google progressively tightened security requirements for third-party Gmail access. Changes to OAuth authentication requirements, stricter API scope limitations, and more rigorous app review processes compounded over time. For a small development team with no ongoing revenue, the compliance burden became insurmountable. When Google required apps to undergo expensive security assessments to maintain API access, Captio could not justify the cost. Simple Memo learned from this and built a completely independent sending infrastructure using Cloudflare Workers and the Resend API. This architecture is not affected by any single email provider's policy changes. You can freely set any email address as your destination -- Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo Mail, iCloud, corporate email, or any other provider.
2. Unsustainable Business Model
Captio used a one-time purchase model -- pay $2.99 once, use forever. While attractive for users, this left developers without ongoing revenue to cover server costs and API maintenance. Since sending emails requires continuous server resources, the one-time model created a structural problem: more users meant greater server costs with no corresponding revenue. After the initial purchase surge slowed, there was simply no money to maintain compliance with evolving API requirements. Simple Memo adopts a freemium model instead. The free plan (3 sends per day) provides unrestricted access to all core features, while the Premium plan ($2.99/month or $29.99/year) unlocks unlimited sends. This recurring revenue stream covers server operating costs, security updates, and ongoing feature development sustainably. A 7-day free trial with unlimited sends is also available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why was Captio discontinued?

Captio was removed from the App Store around 2023 and its servers were eventually shut down. While no official statement was issued, its deep reliance on Gmail API is widely believed to be the primary cause. Google progressively tightened OAuth and API scope requirements for third-party apps, making compliance increasingly difficult and expensive for small development teams. Combined with Captio's one-time purchase business model (which generated no recurring revenue to cover ongoing compliance costs), the situation became untenable.

What is the best Captio alternative in 2026?

Simple Memo is the best Captio alternative available today. It is the only app purpose-built to replicate the exact Captio workflow -- instant launch, one-tap send, auto-clear -- while improving on Captio's weaknesses with AES-GCM encryption, offline Outbox support, and independent infrastructure. Other options like Drafts (more powerful but more complex and expensive) and Apple Notes (free but slow for the email-yourself workflow) exist but serve different primary use cases.

Can I migrate my Captio data to Simple Memo?

Captio had no export feature, so direct data migration is not possible. No export, no import, no data conversion needed.

Will Simple Memo also shut down suddenly like Captio?

Simple Memo was architecturally designed to avoid Captio's failure modes. Yes. Unlike Captio (which was limited to Gmail), Simple Memo works with any email address: Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo Mail, iCloud Mail, corporate email, custom domains, or any other provider. Apple Notes is a full-featured note-taking app with folders, formatting, and attachments -- but emailing a note to yourself takes 4-6 taps and requires navigating through the share sheet each time.
