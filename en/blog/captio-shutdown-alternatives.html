Captio App Shut Down? Here Are the Best Alternatives

By AI Ataka · Published: March 21, 2026 · Reading time: ~10 minutes

Captio App Discontinued

On October 1, 2024, the beloved Captio app was shut down after 14 years of service. For many users, Captio wasn't just a note app -- it was a trusted tool for capturing quick ideas, and its loss felt personal.

The End of an Era: Captio's Shutdown

Captio was remarkable. It did one thing exceptionally well: let you email yourself notes faster than almost any other app. Launched in 2010, it became a favorite among productivity enthusiasts, journalists, writers, and anyone who valued speed and simplicity.

The app's simplicity was its strength -- no unnecessary features, no distracting UI elements, just a text field and a send button. For 14 years, thousands of users relied on Captio as their preferred way to capture fleeting thoughts and send them directly to their inbox.

Why Did Captio Shut Down?

The developer announced in 2024 that maintaining and updating Captio for modern iOS had become increasingly difficult. The app required constant updates to keep up with iOS changes and App Store requirements. Additionally, broader market shifts meant fewer users were willing to pay for a single-purpose app when free alternatives existed.

But here's what the developer said that resonated with users: they never wanted to turn Captio into an ad-filled, bloated, feature-creeping product. Rather than compromise Captio's purity, they decided to retire it on their own terms.

What Made Captio Special?

If you used Captio, you know what made it irreplaceable for many workflows:

  • Lightning-fast capture: From icon tap to sent note in under one second
  • Zero friction: No accounts, no sign-ups, no settings to configure
  • Reliable email delivery: Notes landed in your inbox consistently
  • Beautiful minimalism: The interface was almost invisible -- pure functionality
  • Email integration: Worked as part of a larger email-based workflow
  • Dark mode: Long before iOS added native dark mode, Captio had it

Captio's death left a vacuum. Many users searched desperately for something that could replace it, only to find that each alternative made different trade-offs.

The Top 5 Captio Alternatives for 2026

1. SimpleMemo: The Closest Match

If you're looking for the most direct Captio replacement, SimpleMemo is it. It was literally built by developers who loved Captio and wanted to create something equally focused on speed and simplicity.

Why It's the Best Captio Alternative:

  • Even faster than Captio was (0.3 seconds cold start, 0.15 seconds warm start)
  • Same core concept: one button to email yourself a note
  • Lock Screen widget for fastest possible access
  • Beautiful dark theme that feels natural on modern iPhones
  • No ads, no tracking, no unnecessary features
  • Voice-to-text support for hands-free capture
  • Active development with regular improvements

Considerations:

  • Narrowly focused on email-to-self (not for traditional note-taking)
  • Requires internet for email delivery
  • Subscription pricing (free tier: 3 sends/day; Premium: $2.99/mo or $29.99/yr) versus Captio's one-time purchase ($1.99)

Verdict: If you want the Captio experience modernized for 2026, SimpleMemo is the answer. It captures the same spirit of focused simplicity while adding features that take advantage of modern iOS capabilities.

2. Note to Self Mail

A purpose-built app specifically for emailing yourself notes, "Note to Self Mail" is another strong alternative that emerged after Captio's discontinuation.

Advantages:

  • Very fast (1.2 seconds cold start)
  • Clean, simple interface
  • Free with optional in-app purchase for ad removal
  • Quick template support

Disadvantages:

  • Slower than SimpleMemo
  • Less polished UI compared to Captio
  • Contains ads in the free version

3. Memos: Email Me

Another email-to-self focused app that provides a Captio-like experience with some additional features.

Advantages:

  • Fast loading (1.4 seconds)
  • Supports multiple email addresses
  • Email history within the app
  • Offline mode (saves locally until connectivity returns)

Disadvantages:

  • More complex than Captio (additional features = more UI)
  • Slower speed than SimpleMemo or Captio
  • Requires an account

4. Drafts

While not specifically built for email-to-self, Drafts is a powerful text capture app that can handle Captio's use case while offering far more flexibility.

Advantages:

  • Extremely fast for text capture (0.8 seconds)
  • Powerful automation and scripting
  • Can send emails, but also has 50+ other actions
  • Excellent for complex workflows
  • Active development and strong community

Disadvantages:

  • More complex than Captio (feature-rich can mean more friction)
  • Subscription required for full functionality ($25/year)
  • Overkill if you only need email-to-self
  • Steeper learning curve

Best for: Users who captured notes via email but also used other workflows. Anyone who wants automation power alongside speed.

5. Apple Mail with Shortcuts

The free, no-app-required solution. You can create a Siri Shortcut that accomplishes the same thing Captio did, using Apple's native Mail app.

Advantages:

  • No app to install
  • Uses your existing email account
  • Completely free
  • Customizable to your exact needs

Disadvantages:

  • Slower than purpose-built apps (2-3 seconds)
  • Requires setup time to create the shortcut
  • Less elegant user experience
  • Can be unreliable with Mail app quirks

Best for: Users with minimal budget who are willing to sacrifice speed for free functionality.

How to Migrate From Captio

If you're looking to switch from Captio now that it's gone, here's how to make the transition:

Step 1: Choose Your Replacement

Use the comparison above to pick which app best matches your needs. If speed is your priority, SimpleMemo. If you want free with ads, Note to Self Mail. If you want the most features, Drafts.

Step 2: Set Up Email Configuration

Most of these apps require you to configure your email address once. SimpleMemo does this in settings; others have you enter it during first use.

Step 3: Create a Shortcut (Optional)

If you want the fastest access, create a Lock Screen shortcut or home screen icon. SimpleMemo has a built-in Lock Screen widget which is the fastest way to access it.

Step 4: Delete the Captio App

Since Captio is no longer updated, you can safely remove it from your iPhone. It won't break anything, but you also won't get any support if issues arise with future iOS versions.

What Captio Users Should Know

If you were deeply invested in Captio's ecosystem, you might be wondering about migrating old notes. Unfortunately, there's no direct data migration because Captio notes were stored in your email inbox, not within the app. The good news: all your old Captio emails are still in your inbox. The bad news: you'll need to migrate them manually if you want them in a new note app.

The Silver Lining

Captio's shutdown created space for new, modernized apps to emerge. SimpleMemo and others learned from Captio's model, understanding what made it special, and built upon that foundation. The apps available today are faster, more reliable, and better optimized for modern iOS than Captio ever was. The spirit of "simple, focused, fast" lives on.

Final Recommendation

If you loved Captio for its speed and simplicity, SimpleMemo is the obvious choice. It was built by developers who understood exactly what made Captio great, and they've created something even better.

If you loved Captio's integration with email but also want more features, Drafts provides excellent speed with automation power.

If you're looking for a budget-friendly option, Note to Self Mail provides a solid Captio-like experience for free, though with some ads.

What matters most is that you have options now. The quick-capture email-to-self ecosystem is alive and well in 2026, and the tools available now are faster and more polished than ever before.

Rather than accept that void, they built a modern replacement that honors Captio's legacy while pushing the boundaries of what's possible with iOS optimization. SimpleMemo isn't just inspired by Captio -- it's what Captio would be if it were built fresh in 2026.
