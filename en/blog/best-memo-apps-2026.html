Best Memo Apps 2026
20 Apps Compared: Business, Privacy, Speed

There are countless memo apps, but picking the right one is hard. This article compares 20 leading memo apps along three axes — business, privacy, and speed. A detailed top-10 review plus 10 more apps worth knowing about.

By AI Ataka · Last updated: March 22, 2026

TL;DR

A 2026 overview of leading memo apps along three axes — business, privacy, speed — with a detailed top-10 review and 10 more apps to know about. Business: Notion, Evernote, OneNote, Apple Notes. Privacy: Standard Notes, Joplin, Anytype, Simple Memo (Captio-style). Speed: Simple Memo, Drafts, Apple Notes, Google Keep. Knowledge management: Obsidian, Logseq, Roam Research, Heptabase. Includes pricing, vendor sustainability risk, and benchmark data.

Top 10 Memo Apps for 2026

1. Apple NotesAll-Rounder
Built into every iPhone -- no download needed. Supports text, handwriting, checklists, photo attachments, and PDF markup. Syncs across all Apple devices via iCloud. The 2025 update added AI-powered summaries.
Pros: Free, feature-rich, reliableCons: Apple ecosystem onlyFor: People who live in the Apple ecosystem
2. Google KeepSimple & Light
Free with any Google account. Color-coded notes, labels, reminders, and image OCR. Syncs across Android, iOS, and web. Great for simple notes and lists but not suited for long-form writing.
Pros: Free, lightweight, cross-platformCons: Not for long-form contentFor: People who need simple notes and lists
3. NotionPower User
Notes, databases, project management, and wikis in one app. Block-based editor with flexible layouts and rich templates. However, it's too complex for quick capture and takes 2-3 seconds to launch.
Pros: Incredible flexibilityCons: Slow launch, steep learning curveFor: People who want notes + project management
4. EvernoteKnowledge Manager
The original digital note-taking app with notebooks, tags, full-text search, Web Clipper, and PDF annotation. However, the free tier since 2023 is severely limited (1 device, 50 notes/month). Launch takes 3-5 seconds.
Pros: Web Clipper, full-text searchCons: Restrictive free tier, slow launchFor: People who need to organize large note libraries
5. ObsidianKnowledge Graph
Markdown-based, local-first knowledge base with bi-directional links and a visual knowledge graph. Rich plugin ecosystem. Learning curve is steep and mobile experience is secondary to desktop.
Pros: Bi-directional links, local storage, pluginsCons: Steep learning curve, weaker mobileFor: Researchers and engineers building knowledge systems
6. SimplenoteMinimalist
Completely free plain-text note app by Automattic (WordPress). Markdown support. Syncs across every platform. No ads, no upsells. Minimal but sufficient for pure text notes.
Pros: 100% free, all platformsCons: No images, minimal formattingFor: People who only need text
7. BearDesign-First
Beautifully designed Apple-only note app with Markdown support. Tag-based organization, export options, and focus mode. iCloud sync requires a paid plan. Apple Design Award winner.
Pros: Beautiful UI, MarkdownCons: Apple-only, sync is paidFor: Design-conscious Apple users
8. OneNoteFree-Form Canvas
Microsoft's free digital notebook. Place text, images, and handwriting anywhere on an infinite canvas. Strong Microsoft 365 integration but heavy on mobile.
Pros: Free-form layout, Microsoft integrationCons: Heavy on mobile, complex UIFor: Microsoft ecosystem users
9. Standard NotesPrivacy-First
End-to-end encrypted note app with open-source, audited codebase. Encryption works on the free tier. Paid plans add rich text editors, code editor, and spreadsheets.
Pros: E2E encryption, open sourceCons: Limited free featuresFor: Privacy-conscious users
10. Simple Memo (Captio-style)Instant Capture
Purpose-built for "think it, type it, email it." Launches in 0.3 seconds with cursor ready. Sends memos to your email in 150ms via Cloudflare Workers + Resend API. No account needed, offline Outbox with AES-GCM encryption. Free plan: 3 sends/day, 7-day free trial included.
Pros: 0.3s launch, email delivery, encryptedCons: iOS only, no organization featuresFor: People who need instant capture + email delivery

FAQ

What is the best memo app for 2026?

It depends on your needs. Apple Notes for all-around use, Notion for power users, Simple Memo for instant capture and email delivery, Obsidian for knowledge graphs, Standard Notes for privacy.

Which memo apps are free?

Apple Notes, Google Keep, and Simplenote are fully free. Simple Memo offers 3 free sends/day. Notion is free for personal use. Evernote's free tier is now heavily restricted.

Which is the fastest memo app on iPhone?

Simple Memo (Captio-style) launches in 0.3 seconds with cursor ready. Apple Notes takes about 1 second, Google Keep about 1.5 seconds, Notion 2-3 seconds.

Recommended App Combinations by Use Case

You don't need to pick just one memo app. In fact, the 2026 best practice is combining 2-3 apps by purpose. "Capture" and "organize" require different features, so use the best tool for each job.

Combo 1: Business ProfessionalsProductivity

Capture: Simple Memo → Organize: Notion

Capture meeting insights and on-the-go ideas with Simple Memo and send to email. Then, once a week, move important items from email into a Notion database.

  • Capture speed: 0.3 seconds (Simple Memo)
  • Organization flexibility: databases, kanban boards, calendars for multi-angle management (Notion)
  • Email acts as an intermediate buffer, separating capture timing from organization timing
For: People in meeting-heavy roles who need to link ideas to projects
Combo 2: Researchers & WritersKnowledge Building

Capture: Simple Memo → Organize: Obsidian

Instantly record insights from papers and books with Simple Memo. Use Obsidian's bi-directional links to build a networked knowledge base.

  • Capture speed: 0.3 seconds (Simple Memo) without breaking reading flow
  • Knowledge connection: bi-directional links and knowledge graph visualize relationships (Obsidian)
  • Local storage ensures complete data ownership
For: Researchers and writers managing large volumes of references and ideas
Combo 3: Students & Everyday UseSimple

Capture & Organize: Apple Notes + Google Keep

Use Apple Notes for long-form class notes, and Google Keep for shopping lists, reminders, and quick tasks. Both are free and cross-device.

  • Apple Notes excels at handwriting, PDF markup, and scanning -- ideal for class notes
  • Google Keep's color coding, labels, and reminders are perfect for daily task management
  • Both completely free with no additional cost
For: People who want simple memo management at zero cost
Combo 4: Privacy-Conscious UsersSecurity

Capture: Simple Memo → Organize: Standard Notes

Sensitive ideas and business memos need encryption. Simple Memo protects offline data with AES-GCM encryption, and Standard Notes provides E2E encryption for long-term storage.

  • Simple Memo: encrypted Outbox + zero data retention on servers
  • Standard Notes: E2E encryption + open-source transparency
  • Both have clear privacy policies and no ad-based business model
For: Freelancers and executives handling confidential information

Basic rules for choosing a combination:

  • Capture app requirements: Fast launch (under 1 second), minimal steps (2 taps to start writing), offline-capable
  • Organization app requirements: Powerful search, structural support (folders, tags, links), export functionality
  • Avoid: Trying to do everything in one app. No universal app exists -- you'll end up with compromises everywhere
