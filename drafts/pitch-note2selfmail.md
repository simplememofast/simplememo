Hi Note2SelfMail team,

I'm the developer of SimpleMemo (https://simplememofast.com/), another app in the "email yourself" space. I wanted to reach out because I think we're better off as allies than as silent competitors.

## The Situation

Right now, the "email yourself app" category barely exists in most people's minds. When someone searches for a way to quickly email themselves a note, they mostly find generic advice like "just open Gmail." There's no established category page, no comparison resource, no "best email-to-self apps" roundup that ranks well.

That means we're both competing for scraps of a market that hasn't been properly defined yet. But if the category grows, we both win.

## What I'm Proposing

### 1. Mutual Linking

I'd like to add a "Similar Apps" or "Alternatives" section on SimpleMemo's website that mentions Note2SelfMail as a fellow email-to-self tool. Would you be open to doing the same?

This isn't just goodwill -- it's practical SEO. Cross-linking between legitimate apps in the same niche signals to search engines that the category is real and worth surfacing.

### 2. A Shared "Email Yourself Apps" Comparison Resource

What if we co-created (or independently created and cross-linked) a comparison page? Something like "Email-to-Self Apps Compared" that honestly lists features of both apps (and Captio's legacy, and any others that emerge).

A well-structured comparison page would:
- Rank for "email yourself app" queries that currently have no good result
- Drive traffic to both of our apps
- Establish the category as legitimate in the eyes of media and reviewers

### 3. Category Awareness

If either of us gets media coverage, it helps the other. A journalist who writes about SimpleMemo is more likely to also write about Note2SelfMail, and vice versa. The bigger the category, the more coverage there is to go around.

## About SimpleMemo

For context, SimpleMemo is a free iOS app focused on speed (0.3s launch), encryption (AES-GCM), and offline support. We're positioned slightly differently -- we emphasize the Captio-replacement angle and benchmark performance -- so there's room for both apps without stepping on each other.
