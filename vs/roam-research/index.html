Do I really need Roam Research's graph view?

The graph view is valuable for researchers and writers with 100+ notes connected via bidirectional links who need a bird's-eye view of their knowledge. If your main use is daily idea capture, the graph view is unnecessary, and Simple Memo is far more efficient.

Is Roam's $15/month pricing justified?

For heavy users who create dozens of bidirectional links daily and use block references and queries, yes. If you only take notes a few times a month, Simple Memo's free plan (3 sends/day) offers vastly better value.

Can I replicate Roam's bidirectional links in Simple Memo?

No, Simple Memo has no bidirectional linking. Simple Memo is a "capture" tool; Roam is a "connect" tool. Use both together: capture with Simple Memo, then organize and link in Roam.

When are Roam's block references actually useful?

Block references shine when you need the same text referenced and updated across multiple pages (academic writing, cross-project knowledge management). But learning them takes weeks. For quick notes, they're overkill, and Simple Memo is better suited.

Should I switch from Roam to Logseq or Simple Memo?

It depends on your goal. If you want to continue PKM, free Logseq is a great Roam alternative. If you just need fast capture without PKM, Simple Memo is ideal. Many users settle on Logseq + Simple Memo combined.
